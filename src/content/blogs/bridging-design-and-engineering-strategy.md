Most organizations treat design and engineering as two separate phases of a project. In my experience as a software developer and business owner, this siloed approach is the primary killer of innovation.

### Design is a Business Strategy

Design is not just about aesthetics; it's about the efficiency of intent. A well-designed product reduces the cognitive load on the user, which directly translates to higher retention and lower support costs. 

### The Engineering-Design Feedback Loop

When engineers understand the *why* behind a design, they build more robust systems. Conversely, when designers understand the technical constraints, they can create more feasible and scalable solutions. 

**Key takeaways for founders:**
- Involve engineers in the discovery phase.
- Use Design Systems as a common language between teams.
- Prioritize visual excellence as a marker of technical quality.

The goal is to create products that feel premium not just in their pixels, but in their performance and logic.
